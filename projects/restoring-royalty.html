Restoring Royalty: Red Sandstone Re-construction at Shahpura House, Jaipur

  • Client: Shahpura Palace Heritage Group
  • Expertise: Heritage Restoration & Red Sandstone Reconstruction
  • Stone: Premium Red Sandstone
  • Elements: Jalis, Jharokhas, Arches, Columns, Facade Stonework
  • Location: Jaipur, Rajasthan

Restoring Royalty: Shahpura House, Jaipur — Nirankar Sandstone Industries played a pivotal role in reviving the architectural grandeur of this 19th-century palace. The restoration focused on preserving its royal heritage while strengthening the structural essence using premium red sandstone.

Every element—jalis, jharokhas, pillars, and arches—was meticulously re-crafted by our master artisans to maintain the authenticity of its original design. The project reflects a perfect blend of historical preservation and skilled craftsmanship, ensuring that the palace continues to stand as a proud symbol of Rajasthan’s regal legacy.

What We’ve Done

Our restoration work combined structural revival, aesthetic enhancement, and traditional carving techniques.

  • Red sandstone reconstruction to restore the palace’s original strength and royal architectural form.
  • Handcrafted jali and jharokha installations designed to match historic detailing.
  • Intricate stone elements recreated using traditional Rajasthani carving techniques.
  • Full façade refinement, including arches, columns, and stone cladding.
  • On-site alignment, finishing, and quality control for heritage accuracy.

Preliminary Designs or Layouts

We began by studying existing architectural remnants, old photographs, and physical measurements to map original proportions. Surface patterns, railing profiles, and window modules were recreated through prototypes and sample carvings to ensure historical consistency.

Creative Process

Our process focused on accuracy and authenticity—each carved element was developed through traditional sketches, pattern tracing, and multiple refinement stages. Texture matching and color consistency were prioritized to seamlessly integrate new stone with the old structure.

Building a Successful Client-Architecture Relationship

Restoring heritage spaces requires collaboration and precision. We worked closely with architects, conservation teams, and palace representatives to ensure every detail aligned with historical intentions while meeting restoration best practices.

Project Results

Shahpura House now stands restored to its former glory—an architectural jewel enriched with handcrafted stonework, authentic detailing, and renewed structural strength. The revitalized façade enhances the palace’s hospitality value and preserves its royal charm for future generations.
